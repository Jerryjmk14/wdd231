Quick Nutrition Tips


  • Drink water before meals – It helps control appetite and aids digestion. Sometimes, what feels like hunger is actually thirst.
  • Go for color – A vibrant plate usually means a variety of nutrients. Aim for at least 3 different colors in every meal from fruits and vegetables.
  • Read labels – Watch for hidden sugars and sodium in packaged foods, even in items marketed as "healthy."
  • Choose whole over refined – Swap white rice for brown rice or unpolished local grains like fonio or millet for added fiber and nutrients.
  • Don’t skip meals – Especially breakfast. It jump-starts your metabolism and helps with concentration and energy throughout the day.

Overview of essential nutrients


🥦 1. Carbohydrates: Your body’s main energy source. Found in foods like rice, bread, fruits, and vegetables. Choose complex carbs (like whole grains) for longer-lasting energy.


🍗 2. Proteins: The building blocks for muscles, skin, and organs. They help repair tissues and support growth. Sources include meat, fish, eggs, beans, and nuts.


🥑 3. Fats: Essential for brain health, hormone production, and absorbing vitamins. Healthy fats come from avocados, nuts, seeds, and oils like olive or palm oil (in moderation).


💧 4. Water: Often overlooked, but vital! It regulates body temperature, transports nutrients, and flushes out toxins. Aim to drink plenty throughout the day.


🍊 5. Vitamins: Micronutrients that support immunity, vision, skin health, and more. Each vitamin has a unique role—like Vitamin C for healing and Vitamin D for bones.


🧂 6. Minerals: Help with everything from strong bones (calcium) to oxygen transport (iron). Found in leafy greens, dairy, meats, and whole grains.
